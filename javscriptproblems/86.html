Day 86 JavaScript Challenge — “Factorial Primes”

Taught By Code with Deepanshu Knox

🔍 Problem Of the Day —

A factorial prime is a prime number that is either one more or one less than a factorial.

For example: 5! + 1 = 121 (not prime), 5! - 1 = 119 (not prime), but 3! - 1 = 5 (prime)

Your task is to find all factorial primes less than or equal to a given number n.

🧪 Sample Input:

findFactorialPrimes(100)

🧪 Expected Output:

[2, 3, 5, 7, 23, 719] // All primes that are ±1 of a factorial ≤ 100

🔸 Notes:

  • A factorial is the product of all positive integers ≤ n (e.g. 5! = 5×4×3×2×1 = 120).
  • A number x is factorial prime if either n! + 1 or n! - 1 is a prime.

🧠 Challenge Tip:

  • Use a helper function to check primality.
  • Be careful with large factorials — they grow fast!
